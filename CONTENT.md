# Content Guidelines

## Overview

This document outlines the content standards, structure, and guidelines for creating career guides, cost breakdowns, and other content on the JeetLearnings platform. All content should be accurate, engaging, and tailored for Indian students exploring career options.

## Content Principles

1. **Accuracy**: All information must be factually correct and current
2. **Clarity**: Content should be easy to understand for students aged 14-18
3. **Relevance**: Focus on Indian context, institutions, and opportunities
4. **Engagement**: Use compelling narratives and real-world examples
5. **Completeness**: Provide comprehensive information for informed decision-making
6. **Accessibility**: Avoid jargon; explain technical terms when necessary
7. **Inclusivity**: Represent diverse pathways and backgrounds

## Career Guide Structure

Each career guide follows a standardized 16-section format. This structure ensures consistency and provides comprehensive information for students.

### Section 1: What is This Career All About?

**Purpose**: Introduce the career with an engaging narrative

**Guidelines**:
- Start with a compelling hook or metaphor
- Explain the core purpose of the career in simple terms
- Highlight relevance to India and current opportunities
- Keep to 150-200 words
- Use active voice and present tense

**Example Opening**:
> "Imagine being a detective whose 'crime scene' is a dense tropical forest... A Wildlife Biologist is a professional scientist who studies the biology, behavior, and habitats of wild animals."

### Section 2: A Day in the Life

**Purpose**: Provide realistic, relatable daily activities

**Guidelines**:
- Create a realistic day for a professional in the field
- Include specific times and activities (5:00 AM, 9:00 AM, etc.)
- Show variety: fieldwork, lab work, community interaction, data analysis
- Include 5-7 time blocks throughout the day
- Use first-person narrative or character-based storytelling
- Highlight both challenges and rewards
- Include specific tools, technologies, or locations

**Structure**:
```
5:00 AM - Morning Activity
[Description of what they do]

9:00 AM - Mid-morning Activity
[Description of what they do]

12:30 PM - Midday Activity
[Description of what they do]

3:30 PM - Afternoon Activity
[Description of what they do]

7:00 PM - Evening Activity
[Description of what they do]
```

### Section 3: Is This You? (Personality Traits & Skills)

**Purpose**: Help students self-assess fit for the career

**Guidelines**:
- List 5-7 key personality traits or skills
- Use "Check if you have..." format
- Include both hard and soft skills
- Provide context for each trait
- Use questions to encourage reflection
- Be honest about challenges

**Format**:
```
Check if you have the "Wild DNA":
● Physical & Mental Resilience: Can you stay in a tent for weeks...?
● Extreme Patience: You might wait days just to see...
● Analytical Thinking: Do you love puzzles...?
● Tech-Savvy: Modern biology is high-tech...
● Empathy & Ethics: You must care deeply...
```

### Section 4: Key Responsibilities and Workflow

**Purpose**: Outline daily tasks and work processes

**Guidelines**:
- List 4-6 main responsibilities
- Explain the workflow or cycle of work
- Use action verbs (surveying, analyzing, designing, etc.)
- Include both routine and specialized tasks
- Mention tools or technologies used
- Show how different tasks connect

**Format**:
```
A [Career]'s work follows a [Cycle Name]:
1. [Task]: [Description]
2. [Task]: [Description]
3. [Task]: [Description]
4. [Task]: [Description]
5. [Task]: [Description]
```

### Section 5: Career Pathways in India

**Purpose**: Show educational and professional progression

**Guidelines**:
- Create a clear roadmap from Class 10 to senior positions
- Include specific exams (CUET, GATE, NET/JRF, etc.)
- Mention relevant institutions and certifications
- Show multiple pathways (not just traditional routes)
- Include timelines and milestones
- Mention alternative entry points

**Format**:
```
Entry Point | Roadmap | Milestone
After Class 10 | Choose Science Stream | Strong foundation
After Class 12 | Enroll in B.Sc. | Clear CUET-UG
During Graduation | Take internships | Build "Field Resume"
After Graduation | Pursue M.Sc. | Clear ICAR AIEEA
Research Entry | Qualify for NET/JRF | Become lead scientist
```

### Section 6: Market Snapshot — India 2026

**Purpose**: Provide current salary and demand information

**Guidelines**:
- Include salary ranges for different levels
- Use INR (Indian Rupees) with "Lakhs" notation
- Provide 3-4 salary tiers (Entry, Mid, Senior, Specialized)
- Include growth projections and CAGR
- Cite sources (e.g., "Source: Grand View Research")
- Mention emerging opportunities

**Format**:
```
Salary Ranges (Annual INR)
● Entry-Level: ₹X Lakhs – ₹Y Lakhs
● Mid-Level: ₹X Lakhs – ₹Y Lakhs
● Senior/Leadership: ₹X Lakhs – ₹Y Lakhs
● Specialized Consultant: ₹X Lakhs+ (Common in...)

Growth Projections
The [field] market is expected to grow at a CAGR of X% until 2030
(Source: [Source Name]). This is creating a surge in jobs for...
```

**Guidelines**:

**Format**:

**Purpose**: Provide cost estimates for education

**Guidelines**:
- Include costs for different institution types
- Separate undergraduate and postgraduate costs
- Include living expenses

## Cost Breakdown Structure

Each career includes a detailed cost breakdown with 6-7 categories.

### Cost Categories

1. **Government Institutions**
   - Total 3-year B.Sc. fees
   - Range: ₹15,000 – ₹80,000
   - Include details about fee structure

2. **Private Institutions**
   - Total 3-year B.Sc. fees
   - Range: ₹3 Lakhs – ₹8 Lakhs
   - Include details about different tiers

3. **Masters (M.Sc.)**
   - Postgraduate education costs
   - Mention fellowship opportunities
   - Include duration and total cost

4. **Living Costs**
   - Monthly expenses
   - Range: ₹10,000 – ₹18,000 per month
   - Include major cities

5. **Entrance Exams**
   - CUET, GATE, NET/JRF fees
   - Coaching costs (optional)
   - Study materials

6. **Certifications & Specializations**
   - Professional certifications
   - Specialized training
   - Optional add-ons

7. **Internships & Field Work**
   - Internship costs
   - Field trip expenses
   - Equipment or travel

### Cost Data Format

```typescript
interface CostCategory {
  category: string;              // e.g., "Government Institutions"
  amount: string;                // e.g., "₹15,000-50,000"
  description: string;           // Brief explanation
  icon: string;                  // Emoji or icon
  color: string;                 // Hex color code
  details?: string[];            // Detailed breakdown
}
```

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Tone

- **Professional yet Accessible**: Use clear language without being condescending
- **Encouraging**: Inspire students while being realistic
- **Inclusive**: Represent diverse backgrounds and pathways
- **Authoritative**: Back up claims with data and sources

### Language

- **Avoid Jargon**: Explain technical terms when necessary
- **Use Active Voice**: "You will analyze data" not "Data will be analyzed"
- **Be Specific**: Use concrete examples, not generalizations
- **Use Present Tense**: "A Wildlife Biologist studies..." not "studied"

### Formatting

- **Short Paragraphs**: 2-3 sentences maximum
- **Bullet Points**: Use for lists and key information
- **Bold for Emphasis**: Highlight important terms
- **Consistent Capitalization**: Career titles, institution names

### Examples

**Good**:
> "A Wildlife Biologist studies the behavior and habitats of wild animals. In India, this career is crucial as we work to protect our rich biodiversity. You might track tigers in the Sundarbans or study bird migration patterns in the Himalayas."

**Avoid**:
> "Wildlife Biologists engage in the systematic investigation of faunal behavioral patterns and ecological habitat parameters. This professional discipline is of paramount importance in the Indian subcontinent."

## Data Accuracy Standards

### Salary Information

- Use current 2026 data
- Include ranges, not fixed amounts
- Clearly label entry, mid, and senior levels
- Cite sources (e.g., "Source: Jobted India 2026")
- Update annually

### Institution Information

- Verify current names and locations
- Include admission requirements
- Mention specializations offered
- Update when institutions change

### Scholarship Information

- Verify current eligibility criteria
- Include application deadlines
- Link to official sources
- Update annually

### Market Data

- Use recent reports and studies
- Cite sources with publication dates
- Include CAGR and growth projections
- Mention data collection methodology

## SEO Guidelines

### Keywords

- Include career name in first paragraph
- Use related keywords naturally (e.g., "Wildlife Biologist," "Conservation," "Zoology")
- Include location keywords (e.g., "India," "career in India")
- Use long-tail keywords (e.g., "Wildlife Biologist salary in India")

### Meta Information

- Page title: "[Career Name] - Career Guide | JeetLearnings"
- Meta description: 150-160 characters summarizing the career
- URL slug: lowercase, hyphenated (e.g., "wildlife-biologist")

### Headings

- Use H1 for main career title
- Use H2 for section headings
- Use H3 for subsections
- Include keywords naturally

## Accessibility Guidelines

### Text Accessibility

- Use clear, simple language
- Define technical terms
- Keep sentences short (15-20 words average)
- Use active voice
- Provide context for abbreviations

### Visual Accessibility

- Use descriptive alt text for images
- Ensure sufficient color contrast
- Don't rely on color alone to convey information
- Use semantic HTML structure

### Content Structure

- Use proper heading hierarchy
- Use lists for multiple items
- Break content into scannable sections
- Use white space effectively

## Localization Considerations

### Indian Context

- Use INR for all currency
- Reference Indian institutions and organizations
- Include Indian role models and examples
- Mention Indian government schemes and exams
- Use Indian place names and geography

### Language

- Use Indian English spelling (e.g., "colour," "organisation")
- Use familiar Indian references and examples
- Avoid Western-centric examples
- Include regional diversity
